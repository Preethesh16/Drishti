The Challenge

Ten centers.
None of them can see each other.

A person found at Center B is invisible to a family searching at Center A. The lost person is often elderly or a child, non-literate, panicking, doesn't speak the local language — and usually cannot identify themselves. Names and phone numbers are missing or useless. The two halves of every search never meet.

Silos
Disconnected Centers

Each lost-and-found camp keeps its own paper or local list. A match sitting across the grounds stays unseen for hours or days.

No ID
Weak Signals Only

The lost person can't give a name or number. All you have is age, gender, language, location and a physical description — described differently by everyone.

Many langs
Across Languages

"Saffron saree, rudraksha" and "orange clothes, prayer beads" describe the same person — but no rule-based system connects them.

Our Solution

Match the reports,
not the people

Claude is the brain. Privacy is structural. The whole thing runs offline. Three ideas that work together so no one is surveilled and no match is ever missed.

Claude Cross-Lingual Matching

Claude structures voice intake and matches descriptions across languages — "saffron saree, rudraksha" ↔ "orange clothes, prayer beads" — and explains every match it proposes, so an operator can trust it.

The Brain
Privacy That's Structural

Name and mobile are hashed at ingest. Matching is blind. Real identity is revealed only at a human-confirmed reunion, with an audit log — and raw PII is purged afterward. Privacy is in the architecture, not a checkbox.

Reveal on Confirm
Offline-First, No GPU

The core matcher and the validation number run with pandas and the standard library only — no network, no API key, no GPU. Capture never blocks on the network, so intake works on weak data and dead zones.

Runs Anywhere

The Honest Boundary

What we deliberately
do not do

We don't track the person and we don't scan faces. A human always brings them to a help point — we just make that fast. Owning this boundary is the honest, senior position.

🚫
No facial recognition

The CCTV input is coordinates only — no footage, no faces. We predict where to look, never who is who.

📍
No GPS tracking

The lost person carries no device. We match reports about them, not a live location feed.

🤝
A human always confirms

Identity is revealed only at a confirmed reunion. A blind-spot map places help where separations cluster.

Architecture · One Screen

From a lost report
to a confirmed reunion

Voice-first intake feeds one shared registry. Intelligence runs on top. A human always closes the loop.

1
Intake

An operator asks "Lost someone?" or "Found someone?" Voice-first, landmark-based. Name and mobile are hashed the moment they're captured.

2
The Registry

One shared, live, de-identified pool. Every report — old or new, from any center — lands here and breaks the silos.

3
Intelligence

Tier-1 offline rules plus Tier-2 Claude cross-lingual matching, a drift predictor for where to search, and a blind-spot map for where to place help.

4
Reunite

A targeted alert reaches the nearest help point. A human confirms the match — only then is identity revealed, and the raw PII is purged.
